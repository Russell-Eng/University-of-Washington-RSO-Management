Problem/Data Domain

Student Organizations (RSO) are student held organizations approved by the University that gather people sharing similar interests or goals. For many college students in the University of Washington (UW), participating in registered student organizations (RSO) or attending events held by RSOs constitutes important aspects of their college life. In UW, there are over a thousand RSOs (University of Washington, 2019). While providing students with a vast number of options, the large number of existing RSOs has led to an essential problem. It is easy for students to find information when they know exactly which RSO they are looking for, but what happens if they do not have a pre-knowledge of which RSO to focus on? How can students effectively attain information of these RSOs collectively instead of navigating through over a thousand websites or social media accounts to get to know about the existing RSOs, find potential RSOs to join or target interesting events?

An existing solution to this would be the official Husky Link website by UW (University of Washington, n.d.). The website contains a whole list of existing RSOs and has a page for event seeking. However, the complex event uploading systems resulted in only a few RSOs posting on the website. There is also little space for user interaction on that webpage and little additional information about the events for users to reference other than the official description provided by the RSO itself. These problems hindered the efficiency of information spreading and retrieving and could be improved.

App Description

  • Who are the users of the application? That is, who will be accessing the website?

    Since the website is about organizing uw RSO events, our users will be students and faculties at uw. To prevent other people from viewing the events, the users must log in through their UW accounts before browsing the website.

  • What kind of information will the users be viewing? If you're drawing data form an external source (a csv file or an API), include a link and citation to that data.

    The users are able to view the RSO events that are coming up in the near future. The officers of organizations are also allowed to upload their club’s events to the website, and students and faculties can leave some comments under the events. Therefore, most of the data is generated by the users. However, we will be pulling data from HuskyLink RSO Directory and RSO Forms. The HuskyLink RSO Directory has a list of existing RSOs and some of their events. We will make sure to merge that information to our website in order to keep the website updated. The RSO Forms has all the forms the organizations need to create their own events. For example, an organization will need to fill out the Sales & Food Fundraising Request Form if they are planning to sell items, services or food on campus. Our website will include those forms for the users and select the right form depending on the events they are planning to hold.

  • What will the users do with the web app? What kind of interaction are you expecting to provide? Note that your app will need to support two different interactive features; be sure to explain what both of them are!

    Users will be able to treat the website as a resource of exploring and organizing RSO events efficiently. For students who are interested in joining some events, our website will serve as a clear list with a search engine embedded supporting multiple filter types. Within a few steps of searching, they can check out those events that are closest to their interests based upon key words. For RSO staff, there will be space for uploading events in their RSO mainpage, and every single event can be further described in words and pictures. In addition to searching, the website will hold the “like” button as another kind of interaction, which is for saving interesting events for later.

  • How will using this app help to solve your chosen problem? That is, how is it a solution to the problem you described?

    As described above, the existing solution provided by Husky Link is considered both inefficient in searching and insufficient in content. By the time our web app gets deployed, students will be able to locate a group of events and RSOs that fit their passions and interests the most just by clarifying a couple of types and keywords. Also, RSO staff will find a better place to promote their events and recruit new members through well-developed mainpages and event pages. More people at UW can benefit from this platform that links each other closer socially.
